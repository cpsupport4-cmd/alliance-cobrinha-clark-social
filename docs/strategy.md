## The 4 pillars

### 1. Community Content — daily

Real people, real interactions. Belonging in action.

- Kids helping kids (peer learning)
- Parents watching class (family investment)
- Adults laughing after training (raw vibe)
- Coaches correcting students (the bond is the retention signal)
- Before/after class moments (raw, no music — authenticity > production)

### 2. Member Spotlights — 2–3× per week

Short, raw, honest member stories. Converts better than ads.

- **Feature:** beginners · parents · professionals · kids
- **Format:** "Why I started Jiu-Jitsu" · "What I've learned so far"
- Short. Raw. Honest. No production polish.

### 3. Referral System — ongoing

Don't hope people bring friends. Make it a system.

- **"Train Together" Week** — members can bring 1 friend, 1 parent, or 1 sibling
- **Script:** *"If you've been enjoying training, feel free to invite someone this week."*

### 4. Weekly Community Event — 1× per week

Rotates monthly. Builds retention + connection + referrals.

| Week | Event | Why |
|------|-------|-----|
| 1 | Team Outdoor Activity | Off-mat bonding fuels retention |
| 2 | Kids + Parents Day | Parents train alongside kids — family investment |
| 3 | Fundamentals Workshop | Educational; attracts curious outsiders, retains beginners |
| 4 | Community Roll + Coffee | Post-training social — strongest community-building format |

## 7-day content calendar

The repeating template. Targets per week: **5–7 new inquiries · 3–5 trial bookings · 2–4 conversions.**

### MON — Set the Tone
- **Format:** Class photo or raw video + Story clips
- **Caption tone:** "Start of the week. See you on the mats."
- **Goal:** Consistency + presence

### TUE — Member Story
- **Format:** Short story or quote card. 1 student — adult or parent.
- **Caption tone:** "Started with zero experience. Still here 3 months later."
- **Goal:** Relatability → conversion

### WED — Value / Trust
- **Format:** Coach talking — silent clip with text caption
- **Caption tone:** "Why fundamentals matter" · "What beginners should expect"
- **Goal:** Authority without intimidation

### THU — Kids / Family **(critical)**
- **Format:** Photo or event poster. Kids class moment, parents watching.
- **Caption tone:** Confidence · listening · safe environment
- **Goal:** Parent conversion

### FRI — Community
- **Format:** Raw photo, no voice over
- **Caption tone:** "We train hard, but we enjoy the process."
- **Goal:** Belonging

### SAT — Social Proof
- **Format:** Group photo · packed class · open mat · event poster
- **Caption tone:** "Weekend training with the team."
- **Goal:** FOMO

### SUN — Leader Voice
- **Format:** 15–30 sec silent clip with text caption
- **Caption tone:** Community message — what we're building
- **Goal:** Trust + leadership

### Daily stories — non-negotiable

5–10 every day. Class clips · arrivals · small wins · kids moments · post-training conversations. **Stories are where conversions happen.**

## Content direction

### Do

- More pictures, less text — lead with the image
- Heavy captions in the *visual* sense — short, punchy, warm. 1–2 lines max.
- Event posters > promo creative — make events look exciting, not salesy
- Show the people, the bonds, the laughs
- No voice overs on videos — silence + text captions only
- Real > polished · Faces > empty gym · Interaction > technique · Warm > hype

### Avoid

- Overly technical posts
- Too many promotions
- Generic fitness content
- Long explanations
- Sales-feel language ("Join now", "Sign up", "Limited slots")
